## Types of Power of Attorney We Notarize

### General Power of Attorney

A General POA grants broad authority to your agent to handle financial transactions, sign contracts, manage property, and conduct business on your behalf. This type of POA is typically used when you are traveling or temporarily unavailable.

### Durable Power of Attorney

A Durable POA remains in effect even if you become mentally incapacitated. This is the most common type used for long-term planning. Without the "durable" designation, a POA automatically terminates if the principal becomes incapacitated.

### Limited or Special Power of Attorney

A Limited POA grants authority for a specific transaction or time period. Common uses include authorizing someone to sign closing documents on a real estate transaction, manage a specific bank account, or handle a single business matter while you are unavailable.

### Healthcare Power of Attorney

A Healthcare POA (also called an Advance Healthcare Directive in California) appoints someone to make medical decisions on your behalf if you cannot communicate them yourself. California law requires notarization for these documents.

### Financial Power of Attorney

A Financial POA specifically authorizes your agent to manage your financial affairs including bank accounts, investments, tax filings, and bill payments. Many financial institutions have their own POA forms and may require them to be notarized.
