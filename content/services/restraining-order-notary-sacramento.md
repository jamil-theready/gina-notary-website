## Documents We Notarize for Restraining Orders

### Declarations and Sworn Statements

Courts often require notarized declarations supporting a restraining order petition. These sworn statements describe the incidents and circumstances that justify the order. Gina verifies your identity, confirms you understand the document, and witnesses your signature.

### Proof of Service

After a restraining order is granted, the restrained party must be properly served. The proof of service document confirms that service was completed and is often filed with the court. Gina notarizes proof of service declarations promptly.

### Response Documents

If you have been served with a restraining order and need to file a response, some of the response documents may require notarization. Gina provides the same professional, confidential service to all parties involved.

### Modification and Extension Requests

Restraining orders may need to be modified or extended. These filings can require additional notarized documents. Gina assists with the notarization of modification and extension paperwork.

## Types of Restraining Orders in California

- **Domestic Violence Restraining Order (DVRO)** — protection from abuse by a spouse, partner, family member, or household member
- **Civil Harassment Restraining Order** — protection from harassment, stalking, or threats by someone you do not have a close relationship with
- **Elder Abuse Restraining Order** — protection for adults 65 and older
- **Workplace Violence Restraining Order** — filed by an employer to protect employees
