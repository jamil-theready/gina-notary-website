## Name Change Notarization in Sacramento

Changing your legal name requires properly executed court documents. Whether you are changing your name after marriage, divorce, gender transition, or personal preference, the process involves notarized petitions and declarations that must meet California court requirements.

Gina Gonzalez provides mobile notary services for all types of name change documents throughout Sacramento. As a former lawyer, she ensures your paperwork is executed correctly so your petition is not rejected for technical errors.

## Types of Name Changes We Support

### Marriage Name Changes

After getting married, many people choose to take their spouse's last name or create a hyphenated name. While a marriage certificate is the primary document for this change, additional notarized documents may be needed for banks, employers, the Social Security Administration, and the DMV.

### Divorce Name Restoration

If your divorce decree includes a name restoration order, you can revert to your prior legal name. Gina notarizes any supporting declarations or affidavits needed for government agencies and financial institutions to process the change.

### Court-Ordered Name Changes

For name changes that do not result from marriage or divorce, California requires filing a Petition for Change of Name with the Superior Court. This petition must be signed in the presence of a notary. Gina ensures the petition is properly executed.

### Minor Name Changes

Changing a minor's name requires consent from both parents or legal guardians, unless a court orders otherwise. Gina notarizes the consent forms and petition documents. She can meet both parents separately if they are not available at the same time.

### Gender Identity Name Changes

California provides a streamlined process for name and gender changes. Gina provides respectful, confidential service for all gender identity name change notarizations. She handles these appointments with the same professionalism and discretion she brings to all her work.

## The Name Change Process in Sacramento County

1. **Prepare your petition** — the Petition for Change of Name (form NC-100 for adults, NC-110 for minors)
2. **Get it notarized** — Gina meets you at your location to notarize your signature
3. **File with the court** — submit the notarized petition to Sacramento County Superior Court
4. **Publish the order** — the court may require publication in a local newspaper (exceptions apply for safety or gender identity reasons)
5. **Attend the hearing** — if required, appear before a judge
6. **Update your records** — use the court order to update SSA, DMV, banks, and other institutions
