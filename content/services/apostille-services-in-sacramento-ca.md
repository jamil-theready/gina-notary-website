## What Is an Apostille and When Do You Need One

An apostille is a form of authentication recognized by countries that are part of the Hague Apostille Convention. It verifies that the notary seal or official signature on your document is legitimate. Over 120 countries accept apostilled documents.

You need an apostille when you are using a US document in another country for purposes such as working abroad, getting married in another country, enrolling in a foreign university, conducting international business, or handling inheritance matters overseas.
