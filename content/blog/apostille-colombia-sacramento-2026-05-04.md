## What is an Apostille for Documents Going to Colombia?

An apostille is a specialized certificate. It authenticates the seals and signatures of officials on public documents. It confirms the legitimacy of a document for international use. Colombia is a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention. This international treaty simplifies the legalization of documents. For countries like Colombia, an apostille replaces the more complex process of embassy legalization. You can learn more about the convention from the [US Department of State's apostille information](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/legal/travel-legal-considerations/internl-judicial-asst/authentications-and-apostilles.html).

This means if you have a document issued in California and need it for official use in Bogotá, Medellín, or any other city in Colombia, it requires an apostille from the California Secretary of State.

## Why Do Documents for Colombia Need an Apostille?

Colombian authorities require an apostille to ensure that foreign documents are authentic. This prevents fraud and ensures legal validity. Without an apostille, your California birth certificate, power of attorney, or academic transcript might not be accepted by an official agency in Colombia.

For example, if you are applying for a visa, enrolling in a university, or finalizing a property transfer in Colombia, the relevant government office or institution will request an apostilled document.
