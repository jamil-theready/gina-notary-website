### What are the identification requirements for real estate documents in California?

California law sets strict rules for identifying signers. This protects against fraud in real estate transactions. According to California Government Code Section 1185, a notary public must confirm the identity of each signer. I require valid, unexpired identification. This can be a California driver's license or identification card. A US passport also works. Other approved forms include a foreign passport stamped by USCIS or a military ID. These documents must contain a photograph, physical description, and signature. They must be current or issued within the last five years. I meticulously check each ID. This ensures the integrity of your documents.
