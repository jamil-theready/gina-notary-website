## Why is a Notary Public Essential for Immigration Forms?

Immigration processes involve numerous forms and declarations that demand precision and adherence to strict legal standards. For instance, an Affidavit of Support (Form I-864) for a family based visa application requires the financial sponsor's signature to be notarized. This verification prevents fraud and confirms that the person signing the document is indeed who they claim to be, doing so willingly. ## What Types of Immigration Documents Require Notarization?

While not every USCIS form requires notarization, many crucial documents do. These commonly include:

*   **Affidavits of Support (Form I-864):** Sworn statements by financial sponsors.
*   **Petitions for Alien Relative (Form I-130) supporting affidavits:** Declarations from individuals supporting the relationship.
*   **Declarations and Sworn Statements:** Any document where an individual attests to the truthfulness of facts under oath.
*   **Parental Consent for Minor's Travel:** If a minor is traveling internationally without both parents.
*   **[Power of Attorney for Immigration Matters](/notary-services/power-of-attorney-notary-sacramento/):** Designating someone to act on your behalf.
*   **Translated Documents:** While I provide certified translations, the original document's signer or the translator's affidavit may need notarization.

## The Notarization Process for Immigration Paperwork

Getting your immigration documents notarized is a straightforward process when you are prepared. Here is what to expect:

1.  **Preparation:** Ensure all documents are complete and ready for signature, but do not sign them beforehand. All signers must be physically present.
2.  **Identification:** Each signer must present valid, unexpired government issued photo identification. Acceptable IDs include a California Driver's License or ID card, U.S. passport, or a consular ID card. The name on your ID must exactly match the name on the document.
3.  **Signing:** In my presence, you will sign the documents. I will verify your identity, administer an oath or affirmation if required, and then complete the notary certificate, affixing my official seal.
4.  **Completion:** I return the notarized documents to you, ready for submission.

I often see clients mistakenly sign documents before I arrive, which makes the notarization invalid. Always wait until the notary is present. This small detail can save you a return trip or further delays in your immigration process.
