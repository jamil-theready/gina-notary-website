### Why Is Notarization Crucial for Immigration Documents?

Immigration paperwork often requires notarized signatures to confirm the identity of the signer and the voluntary nature of their act. Documents such as I-130 petitions for family reunification, affidavits of support (Form I-864), declarations, and various consent forms related to green card or naturalization applications must be notarized before submission to the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). Errors or omissions in this process, particularly with identity verification or proper witnessing, can lead to significant setbacks, including requests for evidence (RFEs) or even denial of a visa application. For many immigrant families in Sacramento, accessing reliable and understanding notary services is not just a convenience, it is a necessity for their journey towards legal status, citizenship, or family reunification.

### California Notary Law and Requirements for Immigration Documents

California Civil Code Section 1185 requires that a notary public verify the identity of a signer using satisfactory evidence. For immigration documents, this means presenting a current, unexpired government issued photo identification. This could be a California driver's license or state identification card, a U.S. passport, or in some cases, a foreign passport that has been stamped by USCIS. As your mobile notary, I am legally obligated to confirm your identity thoroughly. It is essential that all documents are complete but unsigned prior to my arrival, as I must witness your signature. I also confirm that the signer appears to understand the document they are signing.
