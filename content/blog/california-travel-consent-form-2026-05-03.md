## Why Do You Need a Travel Consent Form in California?

California state law does not specifically require a travel consent form for a minor to travel without both parents. However, federal laws and international regulations paint a different picture. Many airlines, especially for international flights, and most foreign countries require this document. They do this to prevent child abduction and to ensure that the child travels with proper consent from all legal guardians.

The U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) recommends that a child traveling alone, with only one parent, or with another adult carry a letter of consent. This letter should be signed by the non traveling parent or parents. Without it, you might face delays or denial of travel at the border or airport. Imagine planning a dream vacation to Mexico or Canada, only to be stopped because of missing paperwork. A notarized travel consent form prevents such issues.

## What Exactly is a Travel Consent Form?

A travel consent form is a formal letter. It provides written permission for a minor child to travel without both parents or legal guardians. The form typically outlines who the child is traveling with, the duration of the trip, and the destination. It also gives consent for medical treatment if needed during the journey. This document serves as proof that all parties with parental responsibility approve the travel arrangements.

You need to be very specific when you complete this form. Include the child's full name, date of birth, and passport information. Provide the full names and contact information of the non traveling parents or guardians. Specify the accompanying adult's name and relationship to the child. Detail the travel dates, itinerary, and destination country or countries. A well drafted form leaves no room for questions.

## Do I Need a Notary for a Travel Consent Form California Requires?

Yes, a notary public must witness and certify the signatures on a travel consent form. This adds a layer of legal validity to the document. The notary confirms the identity of the signers and ensures they sign willingly. This crucial step helps prevent fraud and verifies the authenticity of the consent.

California Government Code Section 1185 outlines the requirements for satisfactory evidence of identity for notarizations. I ensure that every signer presents a valid, government issued photo ID. This can be a California driver's license, a state ID card, or a US passport. My role as a notary is to protect all parties involved. I confirm the identity of the signers. ## What Information Should Be on My Travel Consent Form?

A complete travel consent form includes several key pieces of information. Ensure you gather all these details before I arrive for notarization.

1.  **Child's Full Information**: Full legal name, date of birth, place of birth, passport number, and issuing country.
2.  **Accompanying Adult's Information**: Full legal name, passport number, and relationship to the child.
3.  **Non Traveling Parent or Guardian Information**: Full legal names, contact numbers, addresses, and signatures.
4.  **Travel Itinerary**: Specific dates of departure and return, destination country or city, and purpose of travel.
5.  **Medical Authorization**: A clause granting permission for the accompanying adult to seek medical treatment for the child if an emergency arises.
6.  **Emergency Contact Information**: Additional contacts in case of unforeseen circumstances.

Having all this information ready saves time during the notarization appointment.
