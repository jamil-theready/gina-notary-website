## 1. Real Estate and Mortgage Documents

Documents like deeds of trust, loan agreements, and refinance paperwork are legally critical and often require notarization to be valid. A mobile notary can meet real estate agents, buyers, or sellers on-site to finalize transactions smoothly and avoid delays.

## 2. Power of Attorney (POA) Forms

Power of Attorney forms must be notarized in California to be legally binding and enforceable. These documents are commonly used for elderly care, estate planning, or medical decisions, where timing and accuracy are vital.

## 3. Wills and Estate Planning Documents

While California wills typically require witnesses rather than notarization, documents like living trusts, advance healthcare directives, and HIPAA authorizations often do. Proper notarization ensures these papers hold up legally.

## 4. Financial and Legal Agreements

Contracts, affidavits, and settlement agreements all necessitate notarization to verify authenticity and prevent disputes. A certified notary validates these documents, ensuring legal compliance for all parties involved.

## 5. Business and Corporate Documents

From articles of incorporation and operating agreements to partnership contracts, all require proper notarization. Mobile notaries provide the convenience corporate teams need.

## 6. Adoption and Custody Documents

Adoption affidavits, guardianship paperwork, and parental consent forms must be notarized and are subject to rigorous scrutiny. These emotionally important documents demand privacy, sensitivity, and legal correctness.

## 7. Travel and Immigration Documents

Minor travel consent forms and immigration paperwork often require notarization for international travel or visa processing. Timely notarization is especially important for last-minute travel or urgent immigration needs.
