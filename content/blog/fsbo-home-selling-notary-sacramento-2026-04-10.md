## Why Notarization is Critical for FSBO Property Transfers

Many people assume notarization simply makes a document "legal." While it does not validate the content or legality of a document, it provides an essential verification of identity and signature. For property transfers, this step is non negotiable. Notarization protects both the buyer and seller by confirming that the individuals signing the grant deed are indeed who they claim to be, thereby preventing fraud and disputes. ## Key Notary Documents for FSBO Sellers in California

When selling your home FSBO in Sacramento, several documents may require notarization to finalize the sale and legally transfer ownership.

### The Grant Deed

The grant deed is the most fundamental document in any real estate transaction, including FSBO home selling. This document transfers ownership of real property from the seller to the buyer. Under California Civil Code Section 1189, the seller's signature on a grant deed must be notarized before it can be recorded with the Sacramento County Recorder's Office. Without proper notarization, the deed cannot be filed, and the property transfer will not be legally recognized.

### Affidavits of Title

Affidavits of title are sworn statements made by the seller, often confirming that they are the legal owner of the property, that there are no undisclosed liens or encumbrances, and that they have the right to sell. While not always requiring notarization by law, title companies frequently request these to be notarized to add a layer of authenticity and reduce risk. It is a good practice to have any such affidavits notarized to avoid potential delays during closing.

### Power of Attorney (if applicable)

In situations where a seller cannot be present to sign documents, a power of attorney (POA) may be used. This legal document grants another individual, known as the agent or attorney in fact, the authority to sign on behalf of the seller. If a POA is used for an FSBO home selling transaction, the POA document itself must be notarized. Additionally, the agent's signature on the grant deed would then be notarized. I often notarize [power of attorney documents](/notary-services/power-of-attorney-notary-sacramento-guide/) for clients, ensuring the correct protocols are followed.

## The FSBO Notarization Process for Sacramento Homeowners

The process of notarizing your FSBO home selling documents is straightforward, especially with a mobile notary.

### 1. Gather Essential Documents

Before your notary appointment, compile all documents requiring notarization. This typically includes your grant deed, any affidavits, and potentially a power of attorney. Have these documents fully prepared but unsigned. Ensure you have a clear understanding of each document's purpose.

### 2. Present Valid Photo Identification

California notaries are required to verify your identity using specific forms of identification. You must present a current, unexpired government issued photo ID such as a California driver's license, a US passport, or a state identification card. Always bring two forms of ID to any real estate signing just in case. This helps avoid delays.

### 3. Sign in the Notary's Presence

All documents requiring notarization must be signed in front of the notary public. The notary will witness your signature, confirm your identity, and ensure you are signing willingly and knowingly. This step is critical for the notarization to be valid.

### 4. Notary Completes Certificate

After witnessing your signature, the notary will complete the notarization certificate, apply their official seal, and record the transaction in their notary journal. This journal entry is a legal record of the notarization.

### 5. Record the Deed

Once notarized, the grant deed must be recorded with the Sacramento County Recorder's Office. This crucial step legally transfers ownership and makes the transaction a matter of public record. Failure to record the deed promptly can lead to legal complications.

## California Laws and Requirements for Real Estate Notarization

California law is specific regarding the notarization of real estate documents. The primary statute governing the transfer of real property is the California Civil Code. For example, Civil Code Section 1189 specifies the form of the certificate of acknowledgment required for instruments affecting real property. It is important to remember that a notary public verifies signatures, not the legality of the document's content.
