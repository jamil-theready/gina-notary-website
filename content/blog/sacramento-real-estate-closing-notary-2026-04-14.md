## What Does a Real Estate Closing Notary Do?

A real estate closing notary plays a vital role in finalizing property transactions. My main responsibility is to act as a neutral third party. I verify the identities of all individuals signing documents. I witness their signatures. This prevents fraud. It also ensures the integrity of every document.

In California, my duties include confirming the signer's identity through satisfactory evidence. This is mandated by California Government Code Section 1185. It means reviewing government-issued identification. I make sure the person signing is indeed who they claim to be. I also check for complete documents. I ensure all dates and signatures are in the correct places. My notary seal then authenticates the signatures. This makes the documents legally binding.

## What Documents Do I Need Notarized at Closing?

Real estate closings involve a stack of documents. Many of these require notarization. Here are some of the most common ones you might encounter:

*   **Grant Deed:** This document transfers ownership of real property from one party to another. The seller's signature must be notarized to legally convey the property.
*   **Deed of Trust:** For buyers obtaining a loan, this document secures the promissory note. It gives the lender a security interest in the property. The borrower's signature is notarized.
*   **Promissory Note:** This is the borrower's promise to repay the loan. It usually does not require notarization. It is often signed in conjunction with a Deed of Trust.
*   **Loan Documents:** A full loan package often includes dozens of pages. These require notarization. They can include specific disclosures and certifications. As a [loan signing agent in Sacramento](/notary-services/loan-signing-agent-sacramento/), I am fully equipped to handle these complex packages.
*   **Affidavits:** Various affidavits may be required. These are sworn statements of fact. Examples include an Affidavit of Occupancy or an Affidavit of Marital Status.
*   **Power of Attorney:** If one party cannot be present, they might use a [Power of Attorney](/blog/power-of-attorney-notary-sacramento-guide/). The person acting as agent will sign. The original Power of Attorney document itself must have been notarized previously.

I handle each document with precision. My goal is to prevent any issues that could hold up your closing.
