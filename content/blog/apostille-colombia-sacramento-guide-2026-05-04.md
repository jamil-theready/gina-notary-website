## What is an Apostille and Why Does Colombia Require One?

An apostille is a special certificate. It authenticates the seals and signatures of officials on public documents. This makes the documents legally recognized in foreign countries. The process streamlines international document use.

Colombia is a member of the 1961 Hague Apostille Convention. This means countries like Colombia accept an apostille in place of more complex legalization processes. If your California-issued document needs to be legally valid in Colombia, it requires an apostille. Without it, your document may be rejected by Colombian authorities. This includes everything from a birth certificate to a power of attorney.
