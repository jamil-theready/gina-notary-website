### What Are the Identification Requirements for Summer Travel Notary Services?

Proper identification is paramount for any notarization in California. This is a critical step to prevent fraud and ensure legal compliance. California Government Code Section 1185 outlines acceptable forms of identification.

When I notarize your summer travel documents, you will need to present a valid, unexpired government issued photo identification. This typically includes:

*   A California driver's license or identification card.
*   A U.S. passport.
*   An inmate identification card issued by the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation.
*   Any form of identification issued by a U.S. state that is current or issued within five years, contains a photograph, physical description, and signature.
*   A passport issued by a foreign government. It must be stamped by the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).

It is vital that your document is complete but unsigned before I arrive. I must witness your signature in person. This legal requirement protects all parties involved.
