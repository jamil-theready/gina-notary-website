### What Legal Requirements for Marriage are Important in California?

Getting married in California involves specific legal steps. You must first obtain a marriage license. Both parties must apply for the license in person at any County Clerk Recorder's Office. This license is valid for 90 days from the issue date. You must use it within that timeframe. If you do not, it expires, and you need to get a new one.

California law requires the marriage to be solemnized by an authorized person. This includes a priest, minister, rabbi, or judicial officer. A Notary Public who has registered with the California Secretary of State to solemnize marriages can also perform this duty. I am registered to perform this important function.

California Family Code Section 400 outlines who can solemnize marriages. It clearly includes Notaries Public who fulfill specific requirements. This provision ensures the validity of your marriage when performed by a qualified notary. Understanding these requirements from the start saves time and prevents complications.
