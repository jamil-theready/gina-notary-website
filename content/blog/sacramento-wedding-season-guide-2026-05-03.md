## What legal documents require notarization during wedding season in Sacramento?

Many documents associated with marriage benefit from notarization. These are distinct from the marriage license itself.

One common document is a **premarital agreement**, often called a prenuptial agreement. While California Family Code Section 1615 specifies requirements for enforceability, including being in writing and signed by both parties, notarization provides strong evidence of due execution. It helps confirm that each person knowingly and willingly signed the agreement. This step can protect both parties' interests.

Other documents that may need notarization include:

*   **Affidavits for name changes**: If one partner changes their name, an affidavit might be necessary for various purposes.
*   **Property deeds**: If you are transferring property ownership to joint names, a deed requires notarized signatures. This is crucial for real estate transactions, ensuring clear title transfer.
*   **Powers of attorney**: Couples often prepare powers of attorney for health care or financial decisions. These documents designate someone to act on their behalf if they become incapacitated. These require notarization to be legally binding.
*   **Living trusts or wills**: As couples plan their future together, they may update or create estate planning documents. These often require notarized signatures.

## What should couples know about getting a marriage license in Sacramento County?

Securing your marriage license is a vital step before your wedding ceremony. Here is what you need to know for Sacramento County:

1.  **Where to go**: Visit the Sacramento County Clerk Recorder's office.
2.  **Who needs to be there**: Both partners must appear in person.
3.  **What to bring**: You will need valid government issued photo identification, such as a driver's license or passport.
4.  **No blood test**: California does not require a blood test for a marriage license.
5.  **Validity**: Your marriage license is valid for 90 days from the date it is issued. Your ceremony must take place within this 90 day window.
6.  **Type of license**: You can choose between a public or confidential marriage license. A public license is accessible to anyone, while a confidential license requires both parties to have been living together before the marriage and is only accessible by the couple.

I often advise couples to get their license a few weeks before the wedding. This avoids any last minute stress. Remember, your officiant must sign and return the license to the County Clerk Recorder's office after your ceremony to make your marriage legally binding.
