## What Documents Can a Hospital Notary Notarize?

A hospital notary specializes in handling sensitive legal and personal documents that often become necessary in a medical setting. Common documents I notarize include:

*   **Advance Healthcare Directives (AHCDs):** These documents allow individuals to specify their wishes for medical care, appoint a healthcare agent, and make organ donation decisions. California Health and Safety Code sections govern these directives.
*   **Durable Power of Attorney (DPOA):** A DPOA appoints an agent to manage financial or healthcare decisions on behalf of the principal if they become incapacitated. This is governed by the California Probate Code.
*   **Last Will and Testament:** While often drafted in advance, sometimes circumstances require an urgent update or creation of a will.
*   **Medical Releases or Authorizations:** Documents granting permission for information sharing or specific medical procedures.
*   **Affidavits:** Sworn statements of fact, often related to insurance claims or legal proceedings.

I bring all the necessary equipment, including my notary seal and journal, to ensure the process is handled efficiently and securely at your preferred hospital location.

## California Law and Notarization Requirements

California law is very clear on the requirements for notarization, especially when dealing with vulnerable individuals. The primary requirements include:

*   **Positive Identification:** As mandated by California Government Code Section 8202, the signer must present a valid, government issued photo identification. This could be a California Driver's License, a U.S. passport, or an inmate identification card.
*   **Willingness and Awareness:** The notary must confirm the signer is acting voluntarily and understands the nature of the document. If a patient is heavily sedated or unable to communicate clearly, notarization cannot proceed.
*   **Presence of the Notary:** All signers must be physically present before the notary for identification and witnessing the signature.

I adhere strictly to all California notary public laws and regulations to ensure every notarization is legally sound.
