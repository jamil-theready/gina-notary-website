### What Documents Need Notarization When Buying Your First Home in Sacramento?

Several critical documents require notarization during a home purchase. This process provides a layer of legal protection. It ensures the integrity of your transaction.

The most common document is the grant deed. This deed is how property ownership officially transfers from seller to buyer. Without proper notarization, the county recorder cannot accept or record the deed. This delays your ownership. Understanding your [California grant deed](/blog/california-grant-deed-vs-quitclaim-deed-explained/) is fundamental.

### What Identification Do I Need for a Real Estate Notarization in California?

Proper identification is paramount for notarization. California law dictates strict requirements. California Government Code Section 1185 outlines acceptable forms of ID. I must verify your identity. This prevents fraud and protects all parties involved.

You need a current, unexpired government issued photo identification. This often includes:
*   A California driver's license or identification card.
*   A US passport.
*   A passport issued by a foreign government, if it has been stamped by the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services.
*   An inmate identification card issued by the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation.
*   A driver's license issued by another state or Canadian or Mexican public agency, if it includes a photograph, description, and signature.

Make sure your ID is not expired. An expired ID is invalid for notarization. The name on your ID must also match the name on your documents. If there are discrepancies, we need to address them before notarization can proceed. This might involve additional supporting documents.
