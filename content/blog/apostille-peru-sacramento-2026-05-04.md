## What is an Apostille and Why Does Peru Require It?

An apostille is a certificate. It authenticates the seals and signatures of officials on public documents. It makes them valid for use in foreign countries that are members of the Hague Apostille Convention. Peru is a member of this convention. This means any California public document you want to use officially in Peru needs an apostille. Without it, your document may not be accepted.

The purpose of an apostille is to prevent fraud. It confirms that the notary public or other public official who signed or sealed your document is legitimate. This international agreement streamlines the process of legalizing documents across borders. You can find more details about the convention on the [US State Department's Apostille information page](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/legal/travel-legal-considerations/internl-judicial-asst/authentications-and-apostilles.html).

## Which Documents Need an Apostille for Peru?

Many types of documents require an apostille when going from California to Peru. These typically fall into two categories: public documents and private documents.

**Public documents** are issued by government entities. Examples include:
*   Birth certificates
*   Marriage certificates
*   Death certificates
*   Court documents
*   School transcripts and diplomas from public institutions

**Private documents** often require notarization before they can be apostilled. These include:
*   Powers of attorney
*   Affidavits
*   Corporate documents
*   Letters of authorization
*   Single Status Affidavits (for marriage abroad)

If your document is a private one, you must have a California Notary Public notarize it first. I have notarized over 6,000 documents in 10+ years of practice. I ensure proper notarization. This first step is critical for your document's journey to Peru.
